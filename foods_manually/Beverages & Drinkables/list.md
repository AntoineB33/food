Give me a list of categories that together contain all the healthy recipes (and healthy food that can be eaten without being in a recipe in a good meal) in the category "Beverages & Drinkables". An item should not be possibly found in several categories.


To ensure these categories are completely mutually exclusive (meaning a beverage cannot belong to more than one) and collectively exhaustive (meaning all healthy drinks are accounted for), we must categorize them based on their **primary processing method and base composition**. 

To prevent overlap, assume a "hierarchy of complexity." If a drink combines elements from multiple categories (like a smoothie made with milk), it belongs to the most complex category (in this case, Blended Drinks).

Here is the mutually exclusive breakdown of the "Beverages & Drinkables" category for a healthy diet:

### 1. Water & Simple Infusions
This category contains pure water and water that has been lightly flavored by whole, un-blended ingredients. 
* **Defining Rule:** The liquid must be predominantly H2O with no extracted sugars, blended solids, or brewed compounds.
* **Includes:** Flat water, carbonated water (sparkling/seltzer), mineral water, and water infused with intact fruit slices, cucumber, or fresh herb sprigs (e.g., lemon water, mint water).

### 2. Brewed & Steeped Extracts
This category involves using hot or cold water to extract the flavors, oils, and beneficial compounds from dried leaves, beans, or roots, where the physical plant matter is subsequently removed or completely dissolved.
* **Defining Rule:** Contains no added whole foods, milks, or fermentation. The primary flavor comes from extraction via steeping.
* **Includes:** Black coffee, espresso, true teas (green, black, white, oolong), herbal tisanes (chamomile, peppermint), matcha, and steeped ginger or turmeric root water. 

### 3. Savory Drinking Broths
These are liquids extracted from simmering animal or plant materials, characterized by a savory (umami) flavor profile, usually consumed warm.
* **Defining Rule:** Must be savory and intended for standalone drinking rather than as a base for a meal-sized soup containing chunks of food. 
* **Includes:** Bone broth (chicken, beef), clear vegetable broths, and mushroom sipping broths.

### 4. Natural Juices & Plant Extracts
This category is strictly for the natural liquids squeezed or pressed from fruits, vegetables, or plants, where the fibrous bulk (pulp) has been separated and removed.
* **Defining Rule:** Must be liquid extracted from a plant without blending the whole physical structure into the drink.
* **Includes:** Cold-pressed green juices, 100% pure fruit juices (like orange or pomegranate), celery juice, and naturally occurring plant waters (like 100% pure coconut water or aloe vera juice).

### 5. Milks & Emulsions
This includes natural, unsweetened liquids consisting of fats and proteins suspended in water, either derived from animals or extracted from seeds, nuts, or grains.
* **Defining Rule:** Must be an unblended, standalone milk product. If mixed with fruit or powders, it moves to the "Blended" category.
* **Includes:** Unsweetened dairy milk (cow, goat, sheep), and unsweetened plant-based milks (almond milk, soy milk, oat milk, hemp milk).

### 6. Cultured & Fermented Drinks
This category is defined by the introduction of live bacteria or yeast to a base liquid, resulting in a fermented, often probiotic-rich beverage.
* **Defining Rule:** Fermentation is the defining characteristic. Even if it is tea-based or milk-based, the presence of live cultures/fermentation places it strictly in this category.
* **Includes:** Kombucha, milk kefir, water kefir, kvass, and drinking vinegars (like diluted apple cider vinegar).

### 7. Blended Whole-Food Drinks (Smoothies & Shakes)
This category contains beverages made by mechanically blending whole foods or nutritional powders into a liquid, keeping the physical matter, fiber, and macronutrients intact within a thicker drink.
* **Defining Rule:** If a blender is required to puree solid foods, fibers, or functional powders into the liquid, it belongs here. This category acts as the "catch-all" for complex combinations.
* **Includes:** Fruit smoothies, green smoothies, protein shakes, meal replacement shakes, and blended nut-butter drinks.
