Give me a list of categories that together contain all the possible recipes (and food that can be eaten without being in a recipe in a good meal). On item should not be possibly found in several categories.


Categorizing food with absolute mutual exclusivity—meaning zero overlap—is a notoriously difficult challenge. It’s the reason the internet will endlessly argue over whether a hot dog is a sandwich, or if cereal is a soup. Traditional categories like "Appetizer," "Main Course," or "Meat/Vegetable" always overlap because a salad can be an appetizer *or* a main, and a stew contains both meat *and* vegetables.

To achieve your goal of a list where **every edible item fits into exactly one category**, we have to abandon culinary tradition and categorize food by its **Physical Structure and Assembly**. 

Furthermore, to guarantee an item doesn't end up in two categories, we have to use a **waterfall system**. You evaluate a dish from top to bottom; the *first* category it fits into is its only home.

Here is the Mutually Exclusive Culinary Structural Framework:

### 1. Beverages & Drinkables
* **Definition:** Fully liquid items intended to be consumed by drinking from a vessel, without the need for chewing or a utensil.
* **Includes:** Water, wine, coffee, juice, smooth milkshakes, broths served in a mug.

### 2. Suspensions, Soups & Stews
* **Definition:** Dishes where the primary volume is a liquid, sauce, or gravy, and the solid components are submerged, suspended, or entirely coated, requiring a spoon or bowl. 
* **Includes:** Chicken noodle soup, chili, wet curries, oatmeal, and yes—cereal with milk.
* *(If it's too liquid-heavy for a fork, it stops here).*

### 3. Encased, Topped, or Layered Carbs
* **Definition:** Dishes where a baked, fried, or steamed dough/grain base acts as the literal structural container, wrapper, or platform for distinct fillings or toppings.
* **Includes:** Sandwiches, pizzas, tacos, dumplings, pies, burgers, sushi rolls, ravioli, and loaded toast. 
* *(If a carbohydrate is holding or wrapping the other ingredients, it stops here).*

### 4. Noodles & Pastas
* **Definition:** Dishes fundamentally built on long, extruded, or shaped boiled grain/dough matrices that are coated or mixed, but *not* submerged in a pool of liquid, and *not* stuffed.
* **Includes:** Spaghetti bolognese, pad thai, macaroni and cheese, plain buttered noodles.

### 5. Integrated Cooked Mixtures
* **Definition:** Dishes where variously chopped proteins, vegetables, or grains are cooked, mingled, and tossed together uniformly. There is no structural dough wrapper, and no dominant pool of liquid. 
* **Includes:** Stir-fries, fried rice, baked casseroles, breakfast hashes, dry meat-and-veg curries.

### 6. Predominantly Raw Assemblages
* **Definition:** Dishes or plates comprised entirely or almost entirely of uncooked, unheated, fermented, or cured ingredients, occasionally united by a light dressing.
* **Includes:** Green salads, fruit salads, sashimi, charcuterie boards, coleslaw, or a raw apple eaten straight from the tree.

### 7. Unified Baked or Fried Matrices
* **Definition:** Doughs, batters, or processed mixtures that are baked or fried into a single, unified solid state. They do not hold distinct fillings, nor are they tossed with other ingredients.
* **Includes:** A loaf of bread, a plain croissant, an un-iced muffin, plain pancakes, or a plain cake.

### 8. Confections, Gels, & Emulsions
* **Definition:** Non-baked, non-dough foods whose solid or semi-solid structure relies entirely on sugar crystallization, dairy coagulation, freezing, or gelatin. 
* **Includes:** Ice cream, blocks of cheese (eaten alone), chocolate bars, jello, custard, yogurt, and hard candies.

### 9. Isolated Cooked Elements
* **Definition:** The final catch-all. Whole or prominently sectioned pieces of protein, vegetable, or grain that are cooked and served distinctly, without being chopped and mixed into a broader dish. 
* **Includes:** A grilled steak, a piece of roast chicken, a baked potato, steamed asparagus, a fried egg, or a scoop of plain white rice.

***

**Testing the system against edge cases:**
* **Beef Wellington:** Meat wrapped in pastry. Hits Category 3 (Encased Carbs). 
* **A bowl of chili:** Hits Category 2 (Stews).
* **Chili on top of a hot dog:** Hits Category 3 (Topped Carbs) before it can be classified as a stew or an isolated cooked sausage.
